Getting Started

Quick Start — Up in 60 Seconds

1
Open floodsight_v22.html in your browser

Works best in Chrome or Edge. Just double-click the file or drag it into a browser tab. No server, no install, no internet required for the simulation itself.

2
Pick a city from the header pills

Click Tainan, Taipei, Kaohsiung, or Hualien to load real elevation data for that city. The 3D terrain rebuilds instantly.

3
Drag the Water Level slider

On the left panel, move the Water Level slider upward. Watch the 3D terrain flood in real time — buildings submerge, districts change color, the fish swims higher.

4
Toggle to City Map view

Click the Map button in the center panel header. You'll see districts fill with an animated ink-drop wipe, evacuation routes appear, and population data update.

5
Enable Auto-Run for a live demo

Click ▶ Auto Run on the left panel. The flood rises automatically — great for presentations. Click again to stop.

Left Panel

Simulation Controls

Every slider feeds into the real physics engine. Changes propagate across the 3D view, city map, population risk, and AI forecast simultaneously.

💧 Water Level0 – 100 %

The primary flood control. Represents metres above baseline (0–10m). At ~25% low-lying streets flood. At 55%+ most of the basin is submerged. At 80%+ critical alert triggers and Suki-chan turns red.

🌧️ Rain Rate0 – 100 mm/h

Feeds into Manning's equation for discharge (Q = A·R²/³·S½/n). Higher rain adds surface runoff. Above ~50mm/h wind particles appear and wave intensity increases automatically.

💨 Wind Speed0 – 100 km/h

Above ~4 km/h, translucent wind streak particles sweep across both the 3D canvas and city map. At typhoon levels (>80) the streaks become dense and dramatic.

🌡️ Temperature-10 – 45 °C

Below 0°C a cold alert banner appears on the 3D canvas. Used in the Penman-Monteith evapotranspiration equation that affects how fast water drains vs evaporates.

〰️ Wave Intensity0 – 100

Controls how much the water surface oscillates. Higher values create more dramatic foam at the flood boundary, larger ripple rings, and more wave-offset on the water tiles.

💦 Soil Saturation0 – 100 %

Used in the Horton infiltration equation and SCS Curve Number. At 100% the soil is fully saturated — all rain becomes immediate runoff with no absorption, worsening floods dramatically.

▶ Auto Run

Automatically raises the water level from 0 to 100% and loops back. Ideal for presentations, demos, or showing a class the full flood cycle without touching anything. Press again to stop.

⚡ Apply Live Weather

Pulls real-time data from Taiwan's Central Weather Administration (CWA) and syncs all sliders — rainfall, wind, temperature — to actual current conditions outside. Requires internet.

City Locations

Each city uses real SRTM 30-metre Digital Elevation Model data — the same satellite elevation data used by professional GIS systems. The 36×36 grid is bilinearly upsampled to 72×72 for smooth rendering.

🏙️ Buildings

Skyscrapers (Taipei): Steel-blue glass facades, window grids, metal antenna with a blinking red light. Taller buildings have spires.

Regular buildings: Concrete coloring in warm greys, small window dots that are individually lit or dark based on a seed value.

All buildings disappear when submerged by rising water — they're correctly hidden below the flood surface.

✨ Water Caustics

In flooded areas, Voronoi-style light patches swim and shimmer — simulating sunlight refracting through moving water from above.

🔭 Tilt-Shift

The top 30% and bottom 28% of the 3D canvas have soft gradient blur overlays, making the terrain look like a real miniature model.

🌊 Foam Front

White pulsing ellipses appear at the exact boundary where land meets flood — the wave front. Intensity scales with wave intensity slider.

💧 Ripple Rings

Circular rings spawn at random flooded positions, expand, then fade. Frequency and size scale with flood depth.

🌳 Trees

Three types: Pine (stacked triangles), Round (fluffy gradient sphere), Palm (arched fronds). Disappear when flooded.

🛣️ Roads

Roads have a base stripe and animated traveling center-lane dashes. Taipei has 8 roads; other cities have 4. Roads fade when submerged.

⚠️

View Angle slider: At the bottom of the center panel, dragging this rotates the isometric perspective from flat-top (0°) to steep-side (60°). Default is 45° for the classic isometric look.

🗺️
Map View

City Map — All Details

🌊 Ink-Drop Flood Fill

When water rises, each district doesn't instantly change color. Instead, a circular radial wipe grows from the district's center — like an ink drop spreading in water. The wipe edge pulses with an animated shimmer ring.

🚗 Evacuation Routes

Green dashes travel along open routes — the dash speed shows urgency. Amber = caution, slower dashes. Red / static = route blocked by flooding. Updates live as water level changes.

🏥 Shelter Markers

Shelter icons pulse with expanding green rings showing they are active and accepting evacuees. Clicking them shows capacity and status in the evacuation panel.

⚡ Danger Zone Pulses

Severely flooded zones emit double concentric radar ping rings — red for severe, amber for moderate. Ring speed and size scale with how flooded that zone is.

🌡️

Population Heatmap: Toggle the heatmap overlay button to see a cyan gradient layer showing population density. Denser areas glow brighter — this helps identify where flood impact is worst.

Right Panel → Weather Card

Live Weather

How It Works

FloodSight connects to Taiwan's Central Weather Administration (CWA) API on load. The four weather values update in real time. The LIVE badge pulses green when connected.

🌧️ Animated SVG Icons

Each weather field has a hand-crafted animated SVG icon:
• Rain: Three drops fall and loop with staggered timing
• Wind: Three lines scroll left continuously
• Temperature: Sun with 8 rays spinning
• Humidity: Teardrop that pulses/breathes

⚡ Apply to Simulation

The "Apply Live Weather to Sim" button takes the current CWA readings and pushes them directly into all simulation sliders. This lets you simulate the current real-world flood risk for any of the 4 cities.

Right Panel → AI Card

AI Flood Forecast

Powered by Claude AI (Anthropic)

The AI forecast reads your current simulation state — water level, rain rate, wind speed, temperature, city, saturation — and generates a natural-language flood risk assessment using the Anthropic API.

The forecast includes: overall risk level, which districts are most vulnerable, evacuation timing recommendations, infrastructure risk (roads, hospitals, shelters), and a 6-hour outlook.

🤖

Best practice: Set your sliders to a realistic scenario first, then click "Generate Forecast". The AI reads all parameters simultaneously and tailors the forecast to that exact combination.

Right Panel → Population Card

Population & Risk Analysis

📊 District Risk Table

Shows each district with flood depth, risk level (Low / Moderate / Severe), population count, and evacuation priority. Rows highlight red/amber as districts flood. Updates live with every slider movement.

💰 Damage Estimator

Uses Manning's equation discharge values and the SCS Curve Number method to calculate estimated economic damage in USD. The number springs up with a bounce animation when it changes.

Right Panel → Evacuation Card

Evacuation Routing

How Routes Update

As the water level rises, the app evaluates each evacuation route against the current flood extent. Routes that pass through flooded zones automatically switch from green → amber → red (blocked). This happens in real time.

🟢 Open Route

Traveling dashes animate along the road at speed — the motion shows urgency. Fully passable for evacuation.

🟡 Caution Route

Slower dashes, amber color. Passable but conditions are deteriorating. Use with care.

🔴 Blocked Route

Static — no animation. The road is impassable. Shelter markers pulse nearby to show alternate safe zones.

Left Panel → Water Level Card

Water Level Monitor (Tank)

🐟 Blue Fish (Main)

Drawn frame-by-frame using canvas — real S-curve body wave swimming, not just sliding. The spine is computed per frame with sine-wave amplitude increasing toward the tail. Has a dorsal fin, pectoral fin, tail fan, and shiny eye. Turns pink/red at high flood levels. Flips direction correctly using CSS scaleX(-1).

🐠 Orange Clownfish (Companion)

A smaller companion fish swimming at a different speed (3.4s vs 5s cycle) and higher depth position. Orange body with two white vertical stripes, orange tail fan — distinct from the main fish so they feel like actual companions sharing the tank.

💡

The gauge bar below the tank uses a spring animation — when the value changes it overshoots slightly then settles back, just like a real physical gauge. The numeric readout shows metres above baseline.

Visual Charm

Kawaii & Ambient Features

🌸 Sakura Petal Rain

When the flood level is low (below ~25%), up to 8 soft pink cherry blossom petals drift diagonally across the screen from top-left to bottom-right. Each petal is a real 5-petal flower drawn with canvas ellipses and a radial gradient, with gentle sinusoidal sway. As the flood rises the petals fade out and stop spawning — calm gives way to the storm. They return automatically when you lower the water level.

👁️ FloodSight OO Eyes

The two O's in "FloodSight" in the header are real animated kawaii eyes — white eyeball with a blue gradient pupil and a white specular shine. They blink with a scaleY squish every 2 seconds. During AR activation they enter scanning mode. At critical flood levels they cry with animated tear drops falling.

✨ Click Effects

Every click on the page produces: an expanding cyan ripple ring, an inner bloom fill, a radar ping circle, and 6 small sparkle particles that scatter outward with rotation — plus a water droplet sound effect.

🫧 Card Bubbles

Every card in the UI has 8–13 transparent glass bubbles floating upward in the background. Each bubble has a white specular highlight, a gradient interior, and an outer cyan glow — visible as subtle pearl-like spheres drifting behind the content.

🌊 Animated Nav Icons

The bottom navigation icons are styled as glass bubble spheres with white top-left reflections. On hover they morph from circles to pill shapes while keeping the gradient and reflections. The divider between nav sections has a shimmer wave and a small animated 💧 drop.

🎵 Idle Animations (after 30 seconds of no interaction)

Leave the app untouched for 30 seconds and Suki-chan randomly picks one of two special animations:

🌀 Spin Mode

The entire fish rotates continuously on the canvas. Rainbow-colored sparkle symbols (✦) orbit outward as she spins. Lasts 4 seconds then returns to normal.

😺 :3 Cat Face

Eyes become a cat-like arc squint, mouth curves into a small :3 shape, and blush deepens. Cute and disarming. Lasts 4 seconds then returns to normal.

💡

Click anywhere or move a slider to reset the idle timer. The 30-second countdown starts fresh every time you interact with the app.

Ambient Effects

Cursor & Visual Effects

💧 Day Mode Trail

The cursor is a custom 💧 emoji. As you move it, a canvas overlay renders tiny glass water-drop particles that fall behind with gravity. Each drop has a white specular highlight, a cyan-blue radial gradient, and fades out within ~0.6 seconds. Max 9 particles at any time — subtle, not overwhelming.

⭐ Night Mode Trail

Switch to night mode and the water drops transform into twinkling star particles. Each star is a 4 or 5-pointed polygon with a crosshair glow, in randomized blue, purple, and cyan hues. Stars drift instead of falling — they float in all directions with slight momentum.

🖱️ Click Effects — 4 Layers

1. Ripple Ring — An expanding cyan glass ring that grows to 5.5× size and fades over 650ms.

2. Bloom Fill — A simultaneous inner fill that pulses outward from the click point.

3. Radar Ping — A larger radar-style expanding circle in cyan/lavender, fades over 1 second.

4. Sparkles — 6 tiny symbols scatter with rotation (◦ · ° ˚ ∘ ⊙) in all directions.

Header → Report Button

Flood Report Export

What's in the Report

The generated report includes the current simulation timestamp, city name and coordinates, all slider values, the full district risk table with depths and population counts, the economic damage estimate, and the last AI forecast if one was generated. It opens as a print-ready HTML page styled like a formal emergency report.

📄

Pro tip: Generate an AI forecast first, then export the report — the AI text will be included in the document, giving you a complete picture of both the data and the AI's analysis.

Advanced Usage

Pro Tips & Tricks

🎓 For Presentations

Start with Tainan at 0% flood. Enable Auto-Run. Switch to City Map view. The audience watches districts flood in real time with the ink-drop animation. Pause at 60% to discuss the evacuation route status changes.

🔬 For Research

Set Rain Rate to 80 + Saturation to 100 to simulate a worst-case scenario with no soil absorption. Then use "Apply Live Weather" to compare the simulated worst-case against today's actual conditions.

🌀 Typhoon Scenario

Select Typhoon Morakot replay, then manually set Wind Speed to 100 and Rain Rate to 100. The 3D view will show intense wind particles, foam, and wave activity simultaneously with the typhoon eye track.

🌙 Night Demo

Switch to night mode for evening presentations. The starfield background, cyan-glowing cards, and star cursor trail give the app a dramatic mission-control aesthetic that photographs beautifully.

📊 City Comparison

Set Water Level to 40%, note the number of flooded districts in Tainan. Then switch to Taipei and keep the same level — compare how the elevated terrain in Taipei's mountains protects it vs Tainan's flat basin.

🐟 Easter Eggs

Leave the app untouched for 30 seconds to see Suki-chan's idle animation. Click the 💧 drop icon in the header for a kawaii boing sound. Watch the drop icon carefully — it cycles through 10 different facial expressions every 4.5 seconds.
